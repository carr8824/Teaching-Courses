This directory contains advanced learning modules designed for international academic environments (Master's/Ph.D. level) and high-stakes corporate training. 

Drawing from over 12 years of teaching and consulting experience, these modules bridge the gap between academic rigor and actionable business intelligence. The core objective is to equip decision-makers, economists, and data professionals with the tools to move beyond spurious correlations, rigorously quantify impact, and structure evidence-based corporate strategies.

## 📚 Module Catalog & Architecture

The repository accurately mirrors a professional empirical research pipeline, organized into discrete, scalable folders:

### I. Problem Formulation & Research Design
*Rigorous data analysis is useless if the underlying business or research question is flawed. These modules train professionals to structure problems mathematically and conceptually.*
* **[Research Methods_Finding Questions](./Research Methods_Finding Questions/)**: Strategies for identifying high-impact research gaps and translating business pain points into testable hypotheses.
* **[Research Methods_Literature Review](./Research Methods_Literature Review/)**: Systematic approaches to synthesizing existing knowledge, avoiding redundant analyses, and benchmarking industry standards.

### II. Causal Inference & Impact Evaluation
*The gold standard of quantitative analysis. These modules focus on quasi-experimental designs to isolate causal effects from observational data, crucial for accurate policy evaluation and advanced A/B testing in corporate environments.*
* **[Causal Inference_DiD](./Causal Inference_DiD/)**: Difference-in-Differences. Estimating causal effects by measuring the differential change in outcomes across treatment and control groups over time.
* **[Causal Inference_RDD](./Causal Inference_RDD/)**: Regression Discontinuity Design. Exploiting arbitrary thresholds in decision-making rules to identify local causal treatment effects.
* **[Causal Inference_Synthetic Control](./Causal Inference_Synthetic Control/)**: Advanced methodology for comparative case studies, constructing data-driven counterfactuals for strategic interventions (e.g., market entry, regulatory changes).

### III. Applied Strategy & Computational Foundations
*Translating causal models and economic theory into execution.*
* **[Economics for Business_Sustainable Development](./Economics for Business_Sustainable Development/)**: Reframing sustainability from a PR exercise into a hard financial constraint. Navigating the European Green Deal, Double Materiality, and managing ESG risks.
* **[Introduction to R](./Introduction to R/)**:  A foundational programming bootcamp designed to build computational thinking. Moves students from zero to executing robust financial and empirical data pipelines.
