Welcome to the official repository for the **Sustainable Development** course, designed with the analytical rigor required for modern Business Administration and Management.

This is not a traditional ecology course. It is a **business intelligence and risk management** program structured to navigate the European Green Deal and global ESG mandates. The objective is to equip future executives and current managers with the mathematical and strategic capacity to translate planetary boundaries into competitive advantages, eradicate Greenwashing, and optimize the cost of capital (WACC) through robust ESG integration.

---

## 🎯 Program Architecture

The course is structured as a **3 ECTS** (10-week) module with a strict corporate focus, highly scalable to any industrial sector. The methodology disrupts the linear transactional model ("Take-Make-Waste") through:

* **Systemic and Regulatory Thinking:** Mastery of Double Materiality and adaptation to the CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive).
* **Value Chain Engineering:** Transitioning toward "Product-as-a-Service" (PaaS) models and auditing Scope 3 emissions.
* **Finance and Return on Investment (ROI):** Quantifying the impact of sustainability on the P&L and attracting institutional capital.

### 📚 Public Content (Class Material)

The repository is organized into four strategic phases. The slide decks and theoretical frameworks are available below:

#### PHASE I: Foundations and Systemic Thinking
* **Week 1: The Sustainability Imperative & The Growth Paradox.** Systemic risk, Planetary Boundaries, and Doughnut Economics as a model for qualitative development beyond GDP.
* **Week 2: The Regulatory Tsunami.** From the EU Green Deal to corporate duty. Mastering Double Materiality (CSRD/ESRS) and the fundamentals of Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) to avoid regulatory fines.
* **Week 3: Bioeconomy, Blue Frontier & The Strategic Shift.** High-value marine sectors, "Zero Waste" logic, and transitioning from the 4 Ps to the 4 Cs (Customer Solution, Cost, Convenience, Communication).

#### PHASE II: Circular Architecture & Operations
* **Week 4: Circular Economy I (Business Models & Regenerative Design).** The Butterfly Diagram, Cradle-to-Cradle (C2C) principles, and the 9Rs hierarchy to restructure raw material costs.
* **Week 5: Circular Economy II (PaaS & Digital Passports).** Shifting from CAPEX to OPEX via Product-as-a-Service (PaaS) and implementing EU Digital Product Passports (DPP) for supply chain transparency.
* **Week 6: Sustainable Supply Chains & Scope 3 Emissions.** Mapping the GHG Protocol and modeling the financial impact of the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) on B2B procurement.

#### PHASE III: Marketing Strategy
* **Week 7: Sustainable Marketing I (The Attitude-Behavior Gap).** Consumer psychological barriers, Behavioral Economics (*Nudging*), and advanced psychographic segmentation (LOHAS).
* **Week 8: Sustainable Marketing II (Eradicating Greenwashing).** Auditing commercial claims under the EU Green Claims Directive. Leveraging LCA data as a legal shield and a trust-based competitive advantage.

#### PHASE IV: Capital, Reputation, and Impact
* **Week 9: ESG Finance, ROI & The Cost of Capital (WACC).** Integrating ESG scores into asset valuation, the EU Taxonomy for green finance, and aligning CFO-CMO objectives.
* **Week 10: Social Economy, B-Corps & The Risk of Greenhushing.** Calculating Social ROI (SROI), B-Corp metrics, and strategies for radical transparency in the face of legal scrutiny.
